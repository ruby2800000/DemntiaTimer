What is Dementia?

Dementia is a syndrome characterized by a progressive decline in cognitive function, affecting memory, thinking, orientation, comprehension, calculation, learning capacity, language, and judgement. It is not a single disease, but a group of symptoms caused by various disorders affecting the brain.

Common Causes

  • Alzheimer’s disease – The most common cause of dementia.
  • Vascular dementia – Caused by reduced blood flow to the brain.
  • Lewy body dementia – Associated with abnormal protein deposits in brain cells.
  • Frontotemporal dementia – Involves degeneration of the frontal and temporal lobes.
  • Other causes: Parkinson’s disease, Huntington’s disease, infections, head injuries.

Risk Factors

  • Age (risk increases with age)
  • Family history of dementia
  • Chronic diseases (diabetes, hypertension, heart disease)
  • Unhealthy lifestyle (smoking, poor diet, lack of exercise)
  • Head injuries
