Symptoms of Dementia

  • Memory loss – Forgetting recent events, names, or appointments.
  • Difficulty communicating or finding words – Trouble expressing thoughts or understanding conversations.
  • Difficulty with visual and spatial abilities – Getting lost, misjudging distances, or trouble recognizing faces.
  • Difficulty reasoning or problem-solving – Struggling with planning, decision-making, or following instructions.
  • Difficulty handling complex tasks – Trouble managing finances, cooking, or organizing daily activities.
  • Confusion and disorientation – Not knowing the date, time, or location; wandering.
  • Changes in mood or behavior – Increased anxiety, depression, irritability, or apathy.
  • Withdrawal from work or social activities – Losing interest in hobbies, work, or socializing.
  • Impaired judgment – Poor decision-making, risky behavior, or neglecting personal care.

When to Seek Help?

  • If you or a loved one notice these symptoms, consult a healthcare professional for assessment.
  • Early diagnosis can help manage symptoms and improve quality of life.
